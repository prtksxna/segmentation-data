Relations

We are comparing all variables and calculating their Pearson correlation coefficient. It is a measure of linear correlation between two variables.

We have removed Wikidata, Commons and English Wikipedia from the data to get better results.

The first graph just plots the PCC value for all comparisons. We have quite a few that are over 0.75, which means there is a strong linear correlation. The graphs after that plot the two variable, and show the line that best describes the correlation. Wikis that are far away from that line are not following the usual model. The more you scroll, the lesser the correlation.

In general, a correlation can be:

  • Obvious
  • Interesting
  • Incidental
